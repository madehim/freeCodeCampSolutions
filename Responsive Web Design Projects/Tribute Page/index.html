Rutger Oelsen Hauer

The Best Dutch Actor of the 20th Century

Here's a time line of Rutger's life:

  • 23 January 1944 - Born in Breukelen, a town a few miles south of Amsterdam (The Netherlands)
  • 1955 - First role as Eurysakes in the Sophocles tragedy 'Ajax'.
  • 1959 - Run away from home and get on a freighter of the Dutch Merchant Navy
  • 1960 - Back home and attend to school
  • 1962 - Attend to an acting school in Amsterdam, but drop it and join to the Army
  • 1967 - Return to acting school and graduate. Start working with Frisian "Noorder Compagnie"
  • 1969 - Film debute with "Monsieur Hawarden" and gain popularity for the starring role in TV show "Floris"
  • 1973 - Burst onto the European film scene in Verhoeven's "Turkish Delight"
  • 1981 - Start his U.S. "adventure" with role in "Nighthawks"
  • 1982 - Role antihero Roy Batty in "cult-movie" Blade Runner
  • 1985 - Moved to the U.S.
  • 1988 - Receive a "Golden Globe" award for his role as a compassionate Russian officer in the CBS miniseries "Escape From Sobibor"
  • 1994 - Recive a Golden Globe nomination for his role in the HBO film 'Fatherland'. Celebrate his 50th birthday with a retrospective on his most significant films and a long thorough interview/documentary entitled 'Acteur van Oranje'
  • 1997 - Receive the "Rembrandt" award in the Tuschinski Theater in Amsterdam
  • 1999 - Receive the "Best Actor of the Century" award in the Netherlands
  • 2006 - Set up the "Rutger Hauer Film Factory" for gather together young talents in the filmmaking
  • 2007 - Publish a book of "memoirs" entitled "All Those Moments: Stories of Heroes, Villains, Replicants, and Blade Runners"
  • 2013 - Awarded with the "Ridder in de Orde van de Nederlandse Leeuw" ("Knight of the Order of the Netherlands Lion")
  • 19 July 2019 - Passed away at the age of 75
